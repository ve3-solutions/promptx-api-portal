Platform Evolution

PromptX is designed as a long-lived enterprise platform that evolves deliberately, safely, and transparently.

This page explains how PromptX evolves, what customers can expect from change, and how evolution is balanced with stability.

Evolution Philosophy

PromptX evolves according to the following principles:

  • Stability Over Novelty
    New capabilities must not compromise existing integrations or governance.

  • Enterprise-First Change
    Changes are evaluated through the lens of enterprise risk, compliance, and operational impact.

  • Incremental Improvement
    Evolution occurs through small, controlled steps rather than disruptive rewrites.

  • Governance Continuity
    New capabilities inherit existing security, policy, and audit controls by default.

Evolution is intentional, not experimental by default.

What Drives Platform Evolution

Platform evolution may be driven by: - Customer needs and feedback - Regulatory or compliance changes - Security improvements - Reliability and performance optimisation - Advances in AI models and techniques - Operational learnings from real-world usage

Not all drivers result in visible feature changes.

Types of Platform Changes

Non-Functional Improvements

These include: - Performance optimisations - Scalability enhancements - Reliability improvements - Operational tooling updates

Such changes typically do not impact integrations.

Functional Enhancements

Examples include: - New API capabilities - Expanded configuration options - Additional governance controls - New integration patterns

Functional enhancements are introduced cautiously and may be gated.

Experimental or Preview Capabilities

Some features may be introduced as: - Limited availability - Preview or pilot features - Customer-specific trials

These capabilities: - Are explicitly labelled - May change or be withdrawn - Are not guaranteed to become generally available

Backward Compatibility

PromptX prioritises backward compatibility wherever possible.

When backward compatibility cannot be maintained: - Changes follow the documented Deprecation Policy - Customers are provided with advance notice - Migration guidance is supplied where applicable

Backward compatibility is a design goal, not an absolute guarantee.

Relationship to APIs and SDKs

Platform evolution may affect: - API behaviour - SDK capabilities - Configuration options - Operational characteristics

APIs and SDKs evolve in alignment with platform changes but remain subject to their own versioning and deprecation rules.

No Roadmap Commitments

Any references to future capabilities are: - Indicative only - Non-binding - Subject to change

PromptX does not publish or commit to fixed feature delivery timelines through this portal.

This protects customers from reliance on speculative functionality.

Customer Impact and Communication

When changes may affect customers: - Documentation is updated - Changelog entries are published - Targeted communication may be issued for material changes

Customers are encouraged to review documentation regularly.

Customer Responsibilities

Customers are responsible for: - Staying informed of platform updates - Testing integrations against supported versions - Planning upgrades or configuration changes where required

PromptX provides guidance but does not automatically update customer integrations.

Questions About Platform Evolution

If you have questions about how PromptX evolves or how changes may impact your integration, contact the PromptX team.
